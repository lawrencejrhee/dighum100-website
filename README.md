# Comparing Freshman and Transfer Outcomes at UC Berkeley

A Digital Humanities project by Group 4 for DIGHUM 100 with Professor Andressa Maia.

The site compares what happens to students who enter UC Berkeley as freshmen and students who enter as
transfers. It looks at graduation rates, time to degree, graduating GPA, and how transfer students are
spread across majors, and it asks what Berkeley's public data can and cannot tell us about the
experiences behind those numbers.

**Live site:** https://lawrencejrhee.github.io/dighum100-website/

## Group members

Michelle Hong, Jack Hu, Maria Ma, Lawrence Rhee, Isabella Widosh, and Jeddy Xu.

## What's on the site

- **Introduction.** The research question, why it matters, and the theoretical framing (data feminism and visualization ethics).
- **About.** Short bios and an account of who worked on what.
- **Methods.** Where the data came from, how we cleaned it, and the tools we used.
- **Data Critique.** A close reading of what the data includes and what it leaves out.
- **Analysis.** The main narrative, with interactive charts, a historical timeline, and a regional transfer map.
- **Ethics.** Data ethics, the limits of the data, and who the findings affect.
- **Bibliography.** The full list of sources in MLA.

## How it's built

The site is plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with no framework and no build step. The charts are made
with Chart.js, and the regional map is a Tableau Public visualization by Cesar Alesi Perez. It is
hosted on GitHub Pages, so opening `index.html` in a browser is enough to view it.

## Data sources

The Berkeley Data Digest (UC Berkeley Office of Planning and Analysis), the UC InfoCenter, the
California Community Colleges DataVista and DataMart, the California State Auditor, and the National
Student Clearinghouse. Full citations appear on the Bibliography page.
